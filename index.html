Population and Housing Type

Since we know that Sydney has the highest housing price among these major cities, let's take a closer look on how population affects the housing price. This choropleth map shows the population data in 2019, the hue of the region represents the population scale for each local government area. And by clicking the city on the map, we can see a pie chart popped out to show the proportion of different housing types(There are total of three housing categories: unit, townhouse, and House. ) and the total property sales made in 2019. As we can visualize on the map, cities surrounding with darker color block (higher population scale) have higher average housing price (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane), and cities which have lower population has lower housing price (Perth, Canberra, Adelaide).

How does a top university location affect the sales of properties?

Australia University Ranking(Top2000)

In order to figure out how the location of top universities affects house prices, first of all, we need to find out the top university in Australia. The bar chart on the left listed out the universities in Australia which world ranking is within 2000. There is a total of 40 Universities in six major cities, however, one-fourth (9/40) of the top universities are located in Melbourne. So, let’s look closely inside Melbourne, and see whether the location of the university affects the housing market.

Melbourne Average Housing Price (2019)

The map below is a choropleth map of Melbourne, the hue of each suburb represents the average housing price, and the location of each University is also highlighted with red dots on the map. When we zoom in to see the detail, we can discover that most of the top universities are located in Melbourne CBD. However, from the map, we can see that the average housing price near Melbourne CBD are not high. On the contrary, east Melbourne and suburbs along Port Phillip Bay have higher housing prices.

Conclusion

On the first question:

Sydney has the highest housing prices of any major Australian city, and Adelaide has the lowest.


For the second question

The more populous the city, the greater the chance of higher housing prices. In my opinion, due to the limitation of housing resources, with the increase of population, the house price will go up.


One last question:

Based on exploration and visualization, the location of top universities does not directly affect housing prices. However, it can affect house prices more or less, because the suburbs around the top universities have higher than average house prices.
